Steak

My favorite way to cook steak is in a cast iron pan because it retains heat so well. Compared to a non-stick pan or another generic pan, the temperature doesn't drop once you add your steak. This helps you form a good crust on the steak which adds a nice texture. You can certainly cook your choice of steak on a grill, but it might be harder to control the heat.

Things you will need:

  • Steak of your choice, aim for 1.5" thickness
  • Cast iron pan (preferred), any pan will do
  • Olive oil, or any oil of your choice
  • The Trio: garlic salt, black pepper, and garlic powder
  • Patience

Steps:

  1. Allow your steak to come up to temp by taking it out of your fridge 20-30 minutes prior to cooking.

  2. When there are ~5 minutes left, get your pan on the stove and set it to high. *make sure not to add oil during this step or else it will burn*

  3. Pat your steak dry with paper towels to remove any excess moisture, this helps develop a crust.

  4. Add a small amount of oil to help the seasoning stick. Then season with The Trio on both sides, and set it in the pan

  5. Depending on the size, aim for 1-2 minutes per side before flipping. You can check the doneness on your steak by trying to make an indent with your finger. If it leaves an indent, it is still raw->blue. If it springs back extremely fast or it's firm, then it's medium well->well done. If it's somewhere in between, then it's most likely medium rare->medium.

  6. Once you've reached your desired doneness, set aside uncovered for ~10 minutes. This will help the steak relax.

  7. You can now cut, but make sure to cut it against the grain. Look for the grains of the steak and their direction, then cut perpindicular to the direction or in the opposite direction. You're essentially trying to make a + every time you cut. This helps with tenderness.

  8. Enjoy!
